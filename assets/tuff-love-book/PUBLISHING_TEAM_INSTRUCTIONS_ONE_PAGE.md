# TUFF LOVE — Publishing Team Instructions (One Page)

## 1) Source of truth and version control
- **Primary source:** `TUFF_LOVE_COMPLETE_MASTER.md` (this package). Treat it as the authoritative manuscript.
- **Editable working copy:** `TUFF_LOVE_COMPLETE_MASTER_EDITABLE.docx` is provided for markup, copyediting, and layout handoff.
- Please return edits as **tracked changes** (DOCX) or an **annotated PDF proof**, plus a short **change log**. Avoid making changes only inside email threads.

## 2) Interior build targets (baseline)
- **Trim size:** 6 × 9 in
- **Target page count:** 272 pages (final count may move slightly with typography/layout; update spine width accordingly)
- **Margins (baseline):** Inside 0.9 in; Outside 0.7 in; Top 0.7 in; Bottom 0.9 in
- **Typography:** clean hierarchy; chapter titles bold and clear; body text readable (serif or highly legible sans)
- **Pagination:** front matter in **Roman numerals** (i, ii, iii...); main body starts at **page 1**; page numbers typically not shown on title page(s)

## 3) Front matter and back matter handling
- Keep the **FRONT MATTER** section order as delivered in the manuscript.
- Keep the **appendices/Field Kits** intact and easy to navigate with consistent subheads.
- If an **Index** is produced, build it from the final laid out pages, not the manuscript draft.

## 4) Production preflight (must pass)
- Interior PDF export should be press-ready: fonts embedded, images sharp, no missing glyphs
- Convert color once and keep it consistent (avoid multiple CMYK conversions)
- Verify QR codes and URLs render clearly and scan correctly
- Confirm any trademarks (™) and capitalization are consistent

## 5) Deliverables requested back from production
- Print-ready **Interior PDF**
- Print-ready **Cover wrap PDF** (front/spine/back, correct spine width for final page count and paper)
- **Packaged source** (InDesign/affinity package or equivalent with linked assets and fonts)
- Final proof set: **Round 1**, **Round 2**, **Final**

## 6) Approval and sign-off
- Please label proofs clearly (e.g., `R1`, `R2`, `FINAL`) and include a brief change list each round.
- Do not finalize print-ready exports until final proof sign-off is confirmed.
